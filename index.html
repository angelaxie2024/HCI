About Me

Hi! Welcome to my website!

My name is Angela Xie and I am a junior at Northwestern University studying Neuroscience and Computer Science, with aspirations to pursue medicine.

I currently work at the Schmidt Lab studying retinal ganglion cells and their effect on the optokinetic reflex. I am also involved with a multitidue of tutoring and volunteering work. My computer science and tech experiences include two past summer internships.

Outside of school, I love to dance. Growing up, I was trained in ballet, jazz, tap, contemporary, lyrical, and even ballroom! I am in multiple dance groups at Northwestern, focused on hip hop dancing. I often lead and choreograph dances for the largest student-run dance shows on campus.

If you want to check out some of my choreography projects, continue on to the projects section!

Projects

Project 1

Project 1 is a dance that me and my roommate choreographed to "Bloodline" by Ariana Grande

This dance is a hip hop piece that incorporates femme styles.

The cast consisted of 25 dancers that all chose to learn this piece.

Bloodline was performed to sold-out crowds of thousands, as part of Northwestern's largest dance show.

To watch this piece, learn more about the choreography process, or just get more information about this project, click the title "Project 1" to see more.

Project 2

Project 2 is the dance that I choreographed with my friend this year to "We Go Up" by Nicki Minaj.

This dance is also a hip hop piece, but unlike last year, it incorporates both masc and femme styles.

The cast consisted of 17 dancers that learned the choreography over the course of the quarter.

We Go Up was also performed to sold-out crowds of thousands, as part of Northwestern's largest dance show.

Unfortunately, this piece has not been uploaded on youtube and cannot be watched at this time.
